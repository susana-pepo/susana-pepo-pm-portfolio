01 — About

From music therapy
to product management.

I started in children's psychiatry and special education, using music as a therapeutic tool. That work taught me how people actually learn — not from frameworks, but from watching a child find their way through rhythm when words failed them.

Today I bring that understanding to product development at Learnfield GmbH, where I manage product, content, and licensing across two music learning apps — Skoove (piano, 1M+ users) and Trala (violin). I hold three distinct roles simultaneously: Product Owner, Education Content Manager, and License Manager.

My work sits at the intersection of business, creative, and technical domains. In any given week, I might negotiate a multi-year licensing deal with a major publisher, ship an iOS feature that removes onboarding friction, and coordinate a Japanese localization across three parallel workstreams. I like that range. It’s where I do my best thinking.

01

UMPI Licensing Negotiation

Bridging music and product through a €380K deal

Negotiation Financial Modeling Stakeholder Mgmt

Problem

Complex multi-publisher licensing for a music learning app with 1M+ users. Needed to structure a sustainable deal covering free trials, recoupment, and multi-market reporting obligations.

Process

Market share analysis, financial modeling of advance structures, stakeholder alignment across legal, finance, and executive teams. Designed 4-installment payment schedule and recoupment tracking via BMAT.

Outcome

€380K multi-year deal closed. Automated reporting pipeline scoped (3-sprint plan). Cost ratio monitoring built — Jan 2026: licensing costs improved from 18% to 14.7% of revenue.

03

Reducing Onboarding Friction

The No Sign-Up feature that improved App Store sentiment

User Research Agile Delivery Cross-functional

Problem

Mandatory sign-up was creating friction for new users and hurting App Store ratings. User feedback consistently pointed to onboarding as the first barrier to engagement.

Process

Analyzed user feedback and App Store reviews. Scoped the feature removal alongside a paywall fix. Coordinated 2 developers, QA, design, and CPO alignment within sprint cycles.

Outcome

Shipped iOS 7.5.1 with mandatory sign-up removed and paywall optimized. Reduced the first barrier for new users, directly targeting the onboarding friction & App Store sentiment epic.

04

License Planning Financial Model

Bringing product domain knowledge to financial planning

Financial Acumen Process Design Cross-domain

Problem

Finance team couldn't maintain the licensing financial model alone — it required product and content domain knowledge they didn't have. Cost projections were becoming unreliable.

Process

Took over through a structured handover series with Finance (Nov 2025). Built a QA framework with cost-to-revenue ratio checks, customer number validation, and formula audits across 6+ spreadsheet tabs.

Outcome

Self-sustaining financial model with automated checks. Monthly reporting to leadership. Revenue grew from €236K to €315K (Dec ’25 → Jan ’26). Licensing cost share improved: 18% → 14.7%.
